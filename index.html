Project Synopsis

This project explores the use of autoencoders to analyze the consistency of athletes' jumps, focusing on the force-time curves during the jump cycle. Autoencoders, a type of neural network, were applied to identify patterns in force application over time. The primary goal was to normalize jump data based on the moment of takeoff (when force is zero), making it possible to compare jumps across different trials more effectively. Additionally, reconstructed jump profiles were analyzed to assess jump quality and identify potential areas for improvement in an athlete’s performance. The project also aims to link this data-driven analysis with improved training methodologies, potentially guiding better training and recovery strategies for athletes.
